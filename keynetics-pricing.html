Frequently Asked

The Keynetics sensor captures two primary data streams: joint angles and range of motion, which quantifies how far and how accurately joints move through prescribed patterns across sessions; and balance and stability, which measures postural control and weight distribution. These two metrics are critical for injury prevention, rehabilitation benchmarking, and athletic performance optimization.
Both paths give you full access to the platform and all subscription tiers. The Upfront Purchase is $800 paid once, and you receive full trainer commission from day one. The Performance-Based path has no upfront cost — the $1,000 total device cost is paid through a 10% contribution from your earned subscription commissions until the device is paid off, at which point you receive your full commission going forward.
Yes. The Keynetics platform is designed specifically to serve both performance and clinical populations through a single device and platform. Performance clients use Advanced and Elite plans for athlete-grade tracking, while clinical clients use the Elite plan's outcome documentation and care coordination exports. You do not need a separate device or subscription for each population.
Clients subscribe through you — the trainer. You select the appropriate MA tier for each client and enroll them through your Keynetics trainer dashboard. Keynetics handles billing, and your commission is paid to you on a monthly basis. Clients never transact directly with Keynetics.
A Movement Assessment is a structured sensor-based evaluation session that captures joint angle data and balance/stability metrics for your client. The data is processed through the Keynetics analytics platform and generates a session report visible to both you and your client through the progress portal. MAs are the core product unit of the subscription — the more your clients engage, the more measurable their progress becomes.
Yes. Clients can be moved to a higher or lower tier at any time through your trainer dashboard. Tier changes take effect at the start of the next billing cycle. Upgrading clients to Advanced or Elite increases both their assessment frequency and your commission rate — creating a strong incentive to demonstrate progress and recommend upgrades.
The inverse commission model is intentional. Trainers earn a higher percentage as clients commit to higher-frequency plans — 40% at Minimal, up to 70% at Elite. This aligns every incentive: trainers are financially motivated to demonstrate enough progress that clients want to upgrade their plans. Keynetics retains $9–$15 per client per month at every tier regardless, providing consistent platform margin while trainers build meaningful passive income.
Commissions are calculated monthly based on your active subscriber roster and paid out on a rolling 30-day basis. Trainers on the Performance-Based device path will see their 10% contribution automatically deducted until device payoff is reached, after which the full commission rate applies. All commission details are visible in your trainer dashboard in real time.
