Clinical Applications

How physical therapists and sports medicine professionals use the Elite plan.
Keynetics Labs is a performance technology company that delivers sensor-based movement intelligence for personal trainers, fitness professionals, and clinical practitioners. The platform transforms coaching from subjective observation into objective, data-driven performance management through a B2B2C model: trainers acquire the Keynetics device, deploy it with clients, and clients subscribe to structured Movement Assessment (MA) plans.
Keynetics is at the working prototype/MVP stage as of March 2026. Real-time joint angle capture and balance/stability measurement are deployed. Cloud analytics processing is operational. The mobile trainer dashboard and client progress portal are in active development and targeted for release during the current funding cycle.
Two core data streams: (1) Joint Angles and Range of Motion — quantifies how far and how accurately joints move through prescribed movement patterns across training sessions. (2) Balance and Stability — measures postural control and weight distribution, critical for injury prevention, rehabilitation benchmarking, and athletic performance optimization.
Primary: independent personal trainers and small-to-mid-size fitness studios. Secondary: physical therapy clinics, athletic performance centers, collegiate athletic programs, and youth sports development organizations. The hybrid platform serves performance and clinical populations through the same device and software stack.

The Device

Two paths: (1) Upfront Purchase — $800 one-time, full ownership from day one, full commission from day one. (2) Performance-Based Ownership — $0 upfront, $1,000 total cost paid through a 10% contribution from your earned subscription commissions until the device is paid off, at which point full commission applies. Both paths provide identical access to the platform and all subscription tiers.
Manufacturing cost is under $150 per unit at current scale, producing a gross hardware margin of 81%+ on the $800 upfront purchase price. This margin provides strong unit economics from the first device sold and funds the manufacturing cost with significant headroom for operational investment.
The Keynetics sensor is designed for gym-floor deployment — not a clinical lab. Calibration is completed in under 60 seconds at the start of each session. The device connects to the trainer dashboard via the mobile app. Full onboarding support is provided with every device purchase.

Clinical clients use the Elite plan's outcome documentation and care coordination export features. No additional device or software license is required to serve both client types.

Subscriptions

A Movement Assessment is a structured sensor-based evaluation session capturing joint angle and balance/stability data for your client. The data is processed through Keynetics' cloud analytics and generates a session report visible to both trainer and client through the progress portal. MAs are the core subscription unit — subscription tiers are defined by how many MAs are included per month.
Minimal ($15/mo) — 2 MAs, maintenance clients. Moderate ($24/mo) — 4 MAs, general fitness clients. Advanced ($32/mo) — 8 MAs, athletes and active rehab. Elite ($50/mo) — unlimited MAs, high-frequency performers and clinical populations. All tiers include joint angle tracking, balance/stability metrics, and the client progress portal. Higher tiers add session trends, benchmarking, and clinical documentation features. See the Pricing page for a full comparison.
Clients subscribe through you — the trainer. You select the appropriate MA tier for each client and enroll them through your Keynetics trainer dashboard. Keynetics handles billing and delivers the client portal automatically. Clients never transact directly with Keynetics.
Yes. Tier changes are managed through the trainer dashboard and take effect at the start of the next billing cycle. Upgrading clients to Advanced or Elite increases both their MA frequency and your commission rate — creating a direct incentive to demonstrate progress and recommend plan upgrades.
Group licensing is available for gyms, boutique studios, and physical therapy clinics managing large client rosters or multiple practitioners. Contact info@keyneticslabs.com for custom pricing and configuration.

Commission

The commission structure is inverse — trainers earn a higher percentage at higher subscription tiers. Minimal: 40% ($6/mo per client). Moderate: 50% ($12/mo). Advanced: 60% ($19.20/mo). Elite: 70% ($35/mo). Keynetics retains $9–$15 per client per month at every tier. This structure aligns every incentive: trainers are financially motivated to demonstrate enough progress that clients upgrade their plans.
Commissions are calculated monthly and paid on a rolling 30-day basis. Trainers on the Performance-Based device path have a 10% contribution automatically deducted until device payoff, after which the full commission rate applies. All commission details are visible in real time in the trainer dashboard.
A trainer with 20 clients on the Moderate tier earns $240/month ($2,880/year) in passive subscription commission. The same 20 clients on Advanced earns $384/month ($4,608/year). At 30 clients on Advanced, commission reaches $576/month ($6,912/year). These figures are in addition to whatever session fees trainers charge directly to clients — Keynetics commissions are entirely passive income on top of existing revenue.

Clinical Applications

Yes. The Elite tier includes clinical outcome documentation and exportable progress reports designed for care coordination. Keynetics is positioned as a functional outcome measurement tool — not a diagnostic system — making it compatible with PT documentation workflows without triggering clinical regulatory requirements. A clinical advisory program is in development prior to full PT market launch.
Lab-grade systems like Noraxon or Kinesio are priced at $10,000–$30,000 and require controlled clinical research environments. Keynetics is priced at $800 (or $0 upfront via Performance-Based) and deploys on the gym floor or in a PT clinic without specialized infrastructure. The trade-off is precision — Keynetics measures joint angles and balance at session level, not the multi-marker, multi-axis biomechanical analysis of a research lab. For the vast majority of trainer and clinical practitioner use cases, session-level movement data is exactly what is needed.

Pilot partners receive dedicated onboarding support and direct access to the clinical advisory team.
